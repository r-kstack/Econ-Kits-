The problem

A subject that quietly disappeared.

In 1991, economics was the third most popular HSC subject in New South Wales, behind only English and mathematics. Since then, Year 12 enrolments have fallen by around three quarters — and the fall has not been even. Reserve Bank of Australia research shows the decline has been steepest in regional and lower-SES schools, while enrolments have held up in high-SES and selective metropolitan schools.

The RBA's work with educators points to a supply-side cause: too few teachers are equipped to teach economics, so school leaders stop offering it, and students never find out what the subject is. Access to economics — a discipline that shapes who becomes a policymaker, an economist, an informed citizen — has become a function of postcode.

The approach

Fix the constraint, not the symptom.

If the constraint is specialist teaching capacity, the answer is to make the specialism unnecessary. EconKits builds complete, free teaching kits — teacher guides written in plain language for non-economists, slides, worksheets, worked solutions and auto-marked quizzes — so a business studies or HSIE teacher can offer economics with confidence.

Everything is free, forever, under a Creative Commons licence. There is no sign-up, no data harvesting, and no catch. Kits are piloted with regional NSW schools, and pilot feedback decides what gets built next.
