The problem

A subject that quietly disappeared.

In 1991, economics was the third most popular HSC subject in New South Wales, behind only English and mathematics. Since then, Year 12 enrolments have fallen by around three quarters, and Reserve Bank of Australia research shows the decline has been sharply uneven. Indeed, the subject has retreated into a small set of schools while disappearing from most others.

The RBA's work with educators points to a supply-side cause: too few teachers are equipped to teach economics, so school leaders stop offering it, and students never find out what the subject is. Access to economics, a discipline that shapes who becomes a policymaker, an economist, and simply an informed citizen, should not depend on which school you attend.

The approach

Fix the constraint, not the symptom.

If the constraint is specialist teaching capacity, the answer is to make the specialism unnecessary. EconKits builds complete, free teaching kits, with teacher guides written in plain language for non-economists, slides, worksheets, worked solutions and auto-marked quizzes, so that any capable teacher can easily offer economics with confidence, and any student can study it.

Everything is free, forever, under a Creative Commons licence. There is no sign-up, no data harvesting, and no catch. Kits are piloted with Australian schools, and pilot feedback decides what gets built next.

Who's behind it

Built by students, checked by teachers.

EconKits was founded by Ryan, a keen economics student in Sydney, in response to the RBA's research on the decline of economics education. All materials are reviewed by experienced HSC Economics teachers before release.
