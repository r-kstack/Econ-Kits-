The evidence

Economics has become a subject of postcode.

Three decades ago, economics was the third most popular HSC subject in NSW. Today it has largely disappeared from the comprehensive and regional schools most students attend — not because students won't choose it, but because schools can't staff it.

75%

Fall in Year 12 Economics enrolments in NSW since 1991.

RBA, Dwyer (2017)
Low-SES & regional

Where the decline has been steepest — enrolments have held up mainly in high-SES and selective metropolitan schools.

RBA submission to NSW Curriculum Review (2018)
Supply-side

The binding constraint: too few teachers equipped to teach economics, so schools stop offering it.

RBA Bulletin, Livermore & Major (2020)
How it works
