The evidence

Economics has become a subject of postcode.

Three decades ago, economics was the third most popular HSC subject in NSW. Since then, Year 12 enrolments have fallen by around three quarters — and access has narrowed to a small set of schools. Reserve Bank of Australia research points to a supply-side cause: too few teachers equipped to teach economics, so schools stop offering it, and students never discover the subject.

Uneven

The decline has hit some school communities far harder than others, concentrating the subject in a handful of schools.
